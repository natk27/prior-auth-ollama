**STRESS TEST PROCEDURE**

**Procedure Overview**

 Procedure Name: Stress Test
 CPT Code: 93015

A stress test is a diagnostic procedure used to evaluate the heart's ability to function under physical stress, typically achieved through exercise or medication. The purpose of this policy is to outline the coverage criteria and requirements for stress testing services.

**Coverage Criteria (Must Meet All)**

To be covered by our insurance plan, the following criteria must be met:

1. **Conservative Treatment Requirements**: The patient must have undergone 12 weeks of physical therapy for symptoms related to stress test results.
2. **Symptom Duration Requirements**: The patient's symptoms must have persisted for a minimum of 6 weeks prior to the stress test.
3. **Clinical Documentation Required**:
	* A comprehensive medical history, including any previous diagnoses or treatments related to cardiovascular disease.
	* Results from a physical examination, including vital signs and cardiac assessment.
	* Diagnostic test results, such as echocardiograms or electrocardiograms (ECGs).
4. **Imaging Requirements**: The following imaging tests must be performed within the past 6 months:
	* Echocardiogram
	* Electrocardiogram (ECG)
	* Stress test results
5. **Severity Criteria**:
	* Pain scales: The patient's pain level during exercise or stress should not exceed a 3-4 on a scale of 1-10.
	* Functional assessments: The patient's ability to perform daily activities, such as walking or climbing stairs, must be maintained.

**Medical Necessity Criteria**

To determine medical necessity for a stress test, the following criteria must be met:

1. **Patient Selection Criteria**: Stress tests are only recommended for patients with symptoms suggestive of cardiovascular disease, including:
	* Chest pain
	* Shortness of breath
	* Fatigue
	* Palpitations
2. **Failed Prior Treatments**: The patient must have failed conservative treatment, such as physical therapy and lifestyle modifications.
3. **Contraindications to Conservative Care**: Stress tests are indicated for patients with:
	* Severe symptoms, such as syncope or chest pain
	* High-risk cardiovascular disease
	* Unstable angina or coronary artery disease

**Exclusion Criteria**

The following conditions will not be covered by our insurance plan:

1. **Active Infections**: Patients with active infections, such as endocarditis or sepsis, are excluded from coverage.
2. **Pregnancy (if applicable)**: Stress tests are not recommended during pregnancy due to the potential risks to both mother and fetus.
3. **Severe Comorbidities**: Patients with severe comorbidities, such as chronic kidney disease or liver disease, may be excluded from coverage.
4. **Lack of Conservative Treatment**: Patients who have failed conservative treatment, such as physical therapy and lifestyle modifications, are excluded from coverage.
5. **Cosmetic Indications**: Stress tests are not covered for patients with cosmetic indications, such as a desire to improve athletic performance or appearance.

**Documentation Requirements**

The following documentation is required for stress test services:

1. **History and Physical Examination**: A comprehensive medical history and physical examination must be performed by a qualified healthcare professional.
2. **Diagnostic Test Results**: The results of diagnostic tests, such as echocardiograms and ECGs, must be provided to support the diagnosis.
3. **Treatment Logs**: Treatment logs, including physical therapy and lifestyle modifications, must be maintained to document the patient's progress.
4. **Specialist Consultations**: Specialist consultations, such as cardiology or primary care, must be documented to confirm the diagnosis.
