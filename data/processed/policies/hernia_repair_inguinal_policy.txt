PRIOR AUTHORIZATION POLICY
======================================================================

Procedure: Hernia Repair Inguinal
CPT Code: 49505

======================================================================

**Hernia Repair Inguinal (CPT Code 49505) Insurance Prior Authorization Policy**

**PROCEDURE OVERVIEW**

The following policy outlines the requirements for prior authorization of Hernia Repair Inguinal surgery, also known as inguinal hernia repair, using CPT code 49505.

**Clinical Description:**
Inguinal hernia repair is a surgical procedure performed to repair a hernia in the inguinal region. This procedure involves making an incision in the groin area and repairing or reinforcing the weakened muscle wall.

**COVERAGE CRITERIA (Must meet ALL)**

To be eligible for coverage, all of the following criteria must be met:

1. **Conservative Treatment Requirements:**
	* The patient must have undergone at least 12 weeks of physical therapy to manage symptoms and improve function.
2. **Symptom Duration Requirements:**
	* The patient's symptoms must have been present for a minimum of 6 weeks prior to initiation of conservative treatment.
3. **Clinical Documentation Required:**
	* A comprehensive clinical history and physical examination must be documented, including:
		+ Patient demographics and medical history
		+ Symptoms, including pain and discomfort
		+ Physical examination findings, including palpable masses or tenderness in the groin area
4. **Imaging Requirements:**
	* Radiographic imaging (e.g., X-rays, CT scans) must be ordered and interpreted by a board-certified radiologist within 6 months of the patient's date of birth.
5. **Severity Criteria:**
	* The hernia must be classified as Grade 1-3 on the Hartmann grading system, which assesses the severity of the hernia based on its size and symptoms.

**MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA**

To determine medical necessity for Hernia Repair Inguinal surgery, the following criteria must be met:

1. **Patient Selection Criteria:**
	* The patient must have:
		+ Failed conservative treatment (physical therapy) for at least 6 weeks
		+ Experienced significant symptoms, including pain and discomfort, despite conservative treatment
2. **Failed Prior Treatments:**
	* The patient must have undergone failed prior treatments, such as physical therapy or watchful waiting.
3. **Contraindications to Conservative Care:**
	* The patient must be advised against conservative care due to:
		+ Severe symptoms (Grade 4-5 on the Hartmann grading system)
		+ Significant impact on quality of life
		+ Presence of contraindicated conditions, such as deep vein thrombosis or severe comorbidities

**EXCLUSION CRITERIA**

Hernia Repair Inguinal surgery is excluded for coverage if any of the following criteria are met:

1. **Active Infections:**
	* The patient must not have an active infection in the affected area.
2. **Pregnancy (if applicable):**
	* Pregnancy is a contraindication to Hernia Repair Inguinal surgery.
3. **Severe Comorbidities:**
	* The patient must not have severe comorbidities, such as:
		+ Heart disease (Grade 4 or higher on the New York Heart Association classification)
		+ Lung disease (Grade 4 or higher on the New York Heart Association classification)
		+ Renal failure
4. **Lack of Conservative Treatment:**
	* The patient must have undergone at least 12 weeks of conservative treatment before initiating surgery.
5. **Cosmetic Indications:**
	* Hernia Repair Inguinal surgery is not covered for cosmetic indications, such as improving appearance.

**DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS**

The following documentation requirements must be met:

1. **History and Physical Examination:**
	* A comprehensive clinical history and physical examination must be documented by a board-certified physician.
2. **Diagnostic Test Results:**
	* Radiographic imaging (e.g., X-rays, CT scans) must be ordered and interpreted by a board-certified radiologist within 6 months of the patient's date of birth.
3. **Treatment Logs:**
	* A treatment log must be maintained, including:
		+ Dates of treatment
		+ Type of treatment (e.g., physical therapy)
		+ Duration of treatment
4. **Specialist Consultations:**
	* The patient must have consulted with a board-certified surgeon or primary care physician prior to surgery.

**AUTHORIZATION DETAILS**

The following authorization details apply:

1. **Validity Period:** 90 days from the date of approval.
2. **Peer-to-Peer Denial:** If denied, a peer-to-peer review process will be conducted by two independent medical professionals.
3. **Appeal Process:** A patient may appeal a denial within 30 days of receipt of the denial letter.

By following this policy, we aim to ensure that patients receive appropriate and necessary care for Hernia Repair Inguinal surgery while also controlling costs and ensuring quality of care.
