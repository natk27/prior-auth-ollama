**Bariatric Surgery Insurance Policy**

**PROCEDURE OVERVIEW**

* Procedure Name: Bariatric Surgery (CPT Code: 43644)
* Clinical Description: Bariatric surgery is a surgical procedure performed to treat obesity-related conditions, such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, and sleep apnea. The surgery involves reducing the size of the stomach or reconfiguring the small intestine to limit food intake.

**COVERAGE CRITERIA (Must meet ALL)**

1. Conservative Treatment Requirements:
	* Patients must have undergone at least 12 weeks of physical therapy, including exercises targeting core strength, balance, and flexibility.
	* Physical therapy sessions must be documented in patient records, with a minimum of two sessions per week for the duration of 12 weeks.
2. Symptom Duration Requirements:
	* Patients must have experienced symptoms related to obesity for at least 6 weeks prior to authorization request.
3. Clinical Documentation Required:
	* Medical history and physical examination results within the past 6 months, including any relevant lab tests (e.g., lipid profiles, blood glucose levels).
	* Progress notes from primary care physician or specialist consultations regarding patient's weight loss efforts and associated comorbidities.
4. Imaging Requirements:
	* Patients must have undergone a recent body mass index (BMI) calculation within the past 6 months (e.g., most recent visit to primary care provider).
	* A minimum of one abdominal ultrasound or computed tomography (CT) scan with fat content assessment must be documented within the past 12 months.
5. Severity Criteria:
	* Patients must score a minimum of 30 on the Naranjo-Adams criteria for bleeding risk assessment.
	* Functional assessments, such as the Short Form Health Survey (SF-36), must have scores below the 90th percentile.

**MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA**

1. Patient Selection Criteria:
	* Patients with a BMI of 40 or greater or those with a BMI of 35 and at least one obesity-related comorbidity.
	* Inclusion criteria: patients who have failed conservative treatment (physical therapy, lifestyle modifications) for at least 3 months.
2. Failed Prior Treatments:
	* Patients must have documented failure of conservative treatments (physical therapy, lifestyle modifications) for at least 3 months prior to authorization request.
3. Contraindications to Conservative Care:
	* Patients with a history of gastrointestinal surgery or eating disorders.
	* Inclusion criteria: patients who meet the above criteria and have failed conservative treatments.

**EXCLUSION CRITERIA**

1. Active Infections:
	* Patients currently hospitalized for any condition related to obesity (e.g., pneumonia, heart failure).
2. Pregnancy:
	* Pregnant women are excluded from this policy.
3. Severe Comorbidities:
	* Patients with severe comorbid conditions such as kidney disease stage 4 or 5, liver disease stage C, or respiratory disease requiring oxygen therapy.
4. Lack of Conservative Treatment:
	* Patients who have not followed through with at least 12 weeks of physical therapy or other conservative treatments recommended by their healthcare provider.
5. Cosmetic Indications:
	* Patients seeking bariatric surgery solely for aesthetic purposes.

**DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS**

1. History and Physical Examination:
	* Medical history, including any relevant allergies, medications, and comorbidities.
	* Physical examination results within the past 6 months, including vital signs and weight assessment.
2. Diagnostic Test Results:
	* Lab test results (e.g., lipid profiles, blood glucose levels) within the past 6 months.
	* Imaging studies (abdominal ultrasound or CT scan with fat content assessment) within the past 12 months.
3. Treatment Logs:
	* Records of physical therapy sessions and any other conservative treatments provided by healthcare providers.
4. Specialist Consultations:
	* Progress notes from specialist consultations regarding patient's weight loss efforts and associated comorbidities.

**AUTHORIZATION DETAILS**

1. Valid for: 90 days
2. Requires Peer-to-Peer Review if Denied
