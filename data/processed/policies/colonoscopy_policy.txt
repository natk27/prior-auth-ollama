** colonoscopy prior authorization policy **

** Procedure Overview**

The following procedure is being requested:

*   **Procedure Name:** Colonoscopy
*   **CPT Code:** 45378

Colonoscopy is a medical diagnostic procedure where a flexible tube with a camera and light on the end (colonoscope) is inserted through the rectum to visually examine the colon, rectum, and anus for polyps, cancer, or other abnormalities.

** Coverage Criteria (Must meet ALL)**

1.  **Conservative Treatment Requirements:**
    *   Patients must have completed at least 12 weeks of physical therapy prior to authorization for colonoscopy.
2.  **Symptom Duration Requirements:**
    *   Patients must have experienced symptoms for a minimum of 6 weeks prior to authorization.
3.  **Clinical Documentation Required:**
    *   A comprehensive medical history and current health status, including any recent changes or worsening of symptoms.
    *   Results from diagnostic tests such as blood work, imaging studies (e.g., CT scans), and urinalysis, if applicable.
4.  **Imaging Requirements:**
    *   Recent (within the past 6 months) imaging studies to rule out other conditions that may cause similar symptoms (e.g., inflammatory bowel disease).
5.  **Severity Criteria:**
    *   Patients must demonstrate moderate to severe symptoms, as indicated by a symptom score of 5 or higher on the Modified Bowel Symptom Questionnaire (MBSQ).

** Medical Necessity Criteria**

1.  **Patient Selection Criteria:**
    *   Colonoscopy is medically necessary for patients with:
        *   Abdominal pain
        *   Weight loss
        *   Blood in stool or black tarry stools
        *   Changes in bowel habits (e.g., diarrhea, constipation)
        *   Recent gastrointestinal bleeding
    *   Patients who have failed conservative treatment (12 weeks of physical therapy and/or symptom management) for symptoms that may be related to an underlying colon condition.
2.  **Failed Prior Treatments:**
    *   Patients must have completed at least one course of conservative treatment (physical therapy, medication, dietary changes) without improvement in their symptoms.
3.  **Contraindications to Conservative Care:**
    *   Colonoscopy is contraindicated for patients with:
        *   Active infections
        *   Severe comorbidities that may complicate the procedure or recovery (e.g., heart disease, kidney disease)
        *   Recent surgery or medical procedures in the abdominal region

** Exclusion Criteria**

1.  **Active Infections:**
    *   Patients with active infections, such as inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) or infectious colitis.
2.  **Pregnancy:**
    *   Colonoscopy is not recommended during pregnancy due to the risk of complications and radiation exposure.
3.  **Severe Comorbidities:**
    *   Patients with severe comorbidities that may complicate the procedure or recovery, such as heart disease, kidney disease, or liver disease.
4.  **Lack of Conservative Treatment:**
    *   Patients who have not completed a minimum of 12 weeks of conservative treatment (physical therapy and symptom management).
5.  **Cosmetic Indications:**
    *   Colonoscopy is not covered for cosmetic purposes, such as removing polyps for aesthetic reasons.

** Documentation Requirements**

1.  **History and Physical Examination:**
    *   A comprehensive medical history and current health status.
2.  **Diagnostic Test Results:**
    *   Results from diagnostic tests such as blood work, imaging studies (e.g., CT scans), and urinalysis, if applicable.
3.  **Treatment Logs:**
    *   Records of conservative treatment (physical therapy, medication, dietary changes) and response to treatment.
4.  **Specialist Consultations:**
    *   Results from specialist consultations, such as gastroenterology or radiology.

** Authorization Details**

1.  **Valid for:** 90 days
2.  **Requires Peer-to-Peer Review:** If denied, a peer-to-peer review will be required to justify the decision.
