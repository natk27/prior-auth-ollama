Procedure: Upper Endoscopy

**PROCEDURE OVERVIEW**

**Upper Endoscopy**

CPT Code: 43235

**Clinical Description:** An upper endoscopy is a diagnostic procedure used to visually examine the esophagus, stomach, and duodenum (the first part of the small intestine) using a flexible tube with a camera on the end. This procedure helps identify and diagnose conditions such as gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), peptic ulcers, and other gastrointestinal disorders.

**COVERAGE CRITERIA**

To be covered under our insurance plan, the following criteria must be met:

1. **Conservative Treatment Requirements:**
	* Patients must undergo at least 12 weeks of physical therapy to address symptoms such as abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting.
	* Physical therapy should focus on exercises to strengthen the abdominal muscles and improve digestion.
2. **Symptom Duration Requirements:**
	* Patients must have experienced symptoms for a minimum of 6 weeks prior to undergoing upper endoscopy.
3. **Clinical Documentation Required:**
	* A comprehensive medical history, including any previous gastrointestinal procedures or surgeries.
	* A detailed symptom journal documenting the patient's symptoms, including duration and severity.
	* A current medication list, including any over-the-counter medications.
4. **Imaging Requirements:**
	* Patients must undergo a computed tomography (CT) scan of the abdomen within the past 12 months to rule out other conditions such as gastrointestinal cancer or inflammatory bowel disease.
5. **Severity Criteria:**
	* Patients must have a minimum pain score of 3/10 on the National Institutes of Health (NIH) Pain Assessment Scale.
	* Functional assessments, such as the Short Form-36 (SF-36), must demonstrate a minimum improvement in functional status.

**MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA**

To determine medical necessity for upper endoscopy, the following criteria must be met:

1. **Patient Selection Criteria:**
	* Patients with symptoms of GERD or peptic ulcers.
	* Patients with suspected gastrointestinal cancer or inflammatory bowel disease.
2. **Failed Prior Treatments:**
	* Patients who have failed conservative treatment for their condition and require further evaluation.
3. **Contraindications to Conservative Care:**
	* Patients with a history of esophageal stricture or narrowing.
	* Patients with a known allergy to medications used in physical therapy.

**EXCLUSION CRITERIA**

The following conditions will exclude coverage for upper endoscopy:

1. **Active Infections:**
	* Patients with an active infection, such as gastroenteritis or food poisoning.
2. **Pregnancy (if applicable):**
	* Pregnant women may be excluded from coverage due to the risks associated with gastrointestinal procedures during pregnancy.
3. **Severe Comorbidities:**
	* Patients with severe comorbidities, such as heart failure or chronic kidney disease.
4. **Lack of Conservative Treatment:**
	* Patients who have not undergone 12 weeks of physical therapy prior to undergoing upper endoscopy.
5. **Cosmetic Indications:**
	* Upper endoscopies are only covered for diagnostic purposes and not for cosmetic indications.

**DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS**

To support medical necessity for upper endoscopy, the following documentation is required:

1. **History and Physical Examination:**
	* A comprehensive history and physical examination documenting the patient's symptoms and physical status.
2. **Diagnostic Test Results:**
	* The results of the CT scan of the abdomen performed within the past 12 months.
3. **Treatment Logs:**
	* A log of all treatments administered to the patient, including medications and physical therapy exercises.
4. **Specialist Consultations:**
	* Consultation notes from gastroenterologists or other specialists who have evaluated the patient.

**AUTHORIZATION DETAILS**

**Authorization Period:** 90 days

**Authorization Requirements:**

* Prior authorization is required for all upper endoscopy procedures.
* Peer-to-peer review will be conducted if coverage is denied.
* The patient's insurance plan administrator will contact the specialist to discuss the case and determine medical necessity.
