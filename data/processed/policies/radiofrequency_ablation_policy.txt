**RADIOFREQUENCY ABLATION (RFAB) PROCEDURE POLICY**

**PROCEDURE OVERVIEW**

* Procedure Name: Radiofrequency Ablation (RFAB)
* CPT Code: 64635
* Clinical Description: RFAB is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat chronic pain caused by various conditions, including arthritis, kidney stones, and vascular malformations. During the procedure, a specialized device emits radiofrequency energy that heats and destroys targeted tissue, providing relief from pain.

**COVERAGE CRITERIA (Must meet ALL)**

To be covered under this policy, the following criteria must be met:

1. **Conservative Treatment Requirements**: The patient must have completed 12 weeks of physical therapy as an alternative treatment for their condition.
2. **Symptom Duration Requirements**: The patient's symptoms must have persisted for a minimum of 6 weeks prior to the request for RFAB.
3. **Clinical Documentation Required**:
	* A recent history and physical examination dated within the last 30 days, signed by the treating physician.
	* Diagnostic test results (e.g., MRI or CT scans) that demonstrate the presence of the target condition.
4. **Imaging Requirements**: Recent imaging studies (within the last 60 days) must be submitted to confirm the diagnosis and extent of the condition.
5. **Severity Criteria**:
	* Pain scales: The patient's pain level must be at least 5/10 on the Numeric Rating Scale (NRS).
	* Functional assessments: The patient must have a minimum score of 50% on the Oswestry Disability Index (ODI).

**MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA**

To determine medical necessity for RFAB, the following criteria will be evaluated:

1. **Patient Selection Criteria**: Patients who:
	* Have failed conservative treatment (physical therapy and/or pain management).
	* Have a diagnosis confirmed by imaging studies.
	* Meet the severity criteria outlined above.
2. **Failed Prior Treatments**: Patients must have had at least one prior treatment for their condition that was unsuccessful or ineffective.
3. **Contraindications to Conservative Care**: Patients who:
	* Are pregnant or breastfeeding.
	* Have severe comorbidities (e.g., heart disease, lung disease).
	* Have a history of bleeding disorders.

**EXCLUSION CRITERIA**

The following conditions will result in exclusion from coverage:

1. **Active Infections**: Patients with active infections, such as sepsis or osteomyelitis.
2. **Pregnancy (if applicable)**: Pregnant women will not be covered for RFAB due to the risk of adverse effects on fetal development.
3. **Severe Comorbidities**: Patients with severe comorbidities that may increase the risk of complications during or after the procedure.
4. **Lack of Conservative Treatment**: Patients who have not completed 12 weeks of physical therapy as required by this policy.
5. **Cosmetic Indications**: RFAB will not be covered for cosmetic reasons, such as scarring or cosmetic deformity.

**DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS**

To support a request for RFAB, the following documentation must be provided:

1. **History and Physical Examination**: A recent history and physical examination dated within the last 30 days, signed by the treating physician.
2. **Diagnostic Test Results**: Recent imaging studies (within the last 60 days) that confirm the diagnosis and extent of the condition.
3. **Treatment Logs**: Records of all prior treatments, including medications and therapies used to manage symptoms.
4. **Specialist Consultations**: Documentation from specialists consulted regarding the patient's condition and treatment options.

**AUTHORIZATION DETAILS**

* Valid for: 90 days from the date of approval.
* Requires peer-to-peer review if denied.
