**Prior Authorization Policy: Hysterectomy (CPT Code 58150)**

**Procedure Overview**

This policy applies to the prior authorization of hysterectomies for patients with conditions that have not responded to conservative treatment options. The procedure in question is a total abdominal hysterectomy, as described by CPT code 58150.

Clinical Description:
A total abdominal hysterectomy is a surgical procedure that involves the removal of the uterus, cervix, and the upper portion of the vagina. This procedure is typically performed under general anesthesia and takes approximately 2-4 hours to complete. The procedure may be performed for various indications, including but not limited to:

* Uterine fibroids
* Endometriosis
* Cervical cancer
* Chronic pelvic pain

**Coverage Criteria**

To be covered by our insurance policy, the following criteria must be met:

1. **Conservative Treatment Requirements**: The patient must have undergone at least 12 weeks of physical therapy, as prescribed by a licensed healthcare provider.
2. **Symptom Duration Requirements**: The patient's symptoms must have persisted for a minimum of 6 weeks prior to the request for prior authorization.
3. **Clinical Documentation Required**:
	* A detailed medical history and current health status
	* Results from all relevant diagnostic tests (e.g., ultrasound, MRI, or CT scans)
	* Records of all previous treatments, including medications and therapies
4. **Imaging Requirements**:
	* Ultrasound: performed within the past 6 months
	* MRI or CT scan: performed within the past 3 months
5. **Severity Criteria**:
	* Pain scales (e.g., VAS pain scale): 7 or higher on a 10-point scale
	* Functional assessments (e.g., IBS-QOL, FIBQ): score of 60 or lower

**Medical Necessity Criteria**

To determine medical necessity for this procedure, we will consider the following:

1. **Patient Selection Criteria**: The patient must have:
	* Failed conservative treatment options (physical therapy, medications, and other non-surgical interventions)
	* A diagnosis that has been confirmed by a licensed healthcare provider
2. **Failed Prior Treatments**:
	* The patient's symptoms have persisted despite at least 12 weeks of physical therapy
	* Previous treatments, including medications and therapies, have not provided adequate relief
3. **Contraindications to Conservative Care**: The patient must have:
	* Severe pain that interferes with daily activities (VAS pain scale â‰¥ 7)
	* Significant functional impairment (e.g., difficulty with bowel or bladder control)

**Exclusion Criteria**

The following conditions will exclude a patient from prior authorization for this procedure:

1. **Active Infections**: The patient must be free of active infections, including but not limited to pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), endometritis, or sepsis.
2. **Pregnancy (if applicable)**: The patient must be postpartum and have completed any necessary treatment for pregnancy-related complications.
3. **Severe Comorbidities**: The patient must not have a severe comorbidity that would increase the risk of surgery (e.g., uncontrolled hypertension, diabetes, or cardiovascular disease).
4. **Lack of Conservative Treatment**: The patient must have undergone at least 12 weeks of physical therapy and other conservative treatments.
5. **Cosmetic Indications**: The procedure is not covered for cosmetic purposes.

**Documentation Requirements**

To support prior authorization for this procedure, the following documentation is required:

1. **History and Physical Examination**: A comprehensive medical history and current health status, as documented in the patient's medical record.
2. **Diagnostic Test Results**: Copies of all relevant diagnostic tests (e.g., ultrasound, MRI, or CT scans) performed within the past 6 months.
3. **Treatment Logs**: Records of all previous treatments, including medications and therapies.
4. **Specialist Consultations**: A letter or report from a licensed healthcare provider detailing their assessment of the patient's condition.

**Authorization Details**

1. **Valid for**: This policy is valid for 90 days from the date of prior authorization.
2. **Requires Peer-to-Peer Review**: If prior authorization is denied, the request must be reviewed by a peer-to-peer reviewer to ensure that the decision is consistent with our policy and applicable laws and regulations.
