Procedure: MRI Knee
CPT Code: 73721

======================================================================

**MRI Knee Prior Authorization Policy**

**Procedure Overview**

The following policy applies to the provision of Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) services for knee-related conditions, specifically CPT code 73721.

**Clinical Description**
The MRI procedure involves the use of a magnetic field and radio waves to produce detailed images of the knee joint. This diagnostic imaging service is used to evaluate and monitor various knee-related conditions, including osteoarthritis, ligament sprains, and meniscal tears.

**Coverage Criteria (Must Meet All)**

To be covered under this policy, the following criteria must be met:

1. **Conservative Treatment Requirements**: The patient must have attempted conservative treatment for a minimum of 12 weeks, including:
	* Physical therapy sessions (minimum 2 times per week)
	* Pain management medication (prescription strength)
	* Lifestyle modifications (e.g., weight loss, exercise modification)
2. **Symptom Duration Requirements**:
	* The patient must have experienced knee pain or symptoms for a minimum of 6 weeks.
	* The duration of symptoms must be documented in the patient's medical record.
3. **Clinical Documentation Required**: The following documentation is required to support the need for an MRI:
	* A detailed history and physical examination performed by the treating physician
	* Diagnostic test results (e.g., X-rays, EMG)
	* Treatment logs documenting conservative treatment efforts
	* Specialist consultations with orthopedic or rheumatology specialists
4. **Imaging Requirements**:
	* The MRI must be ordered by a licensed physician.
	* The imaging service provider must have expertise in musculoskeletal imaging.
5. **Severity Criteria**: The patient's condition must meet the following severity criteria:
	* Pain scales: 6 or higher on a 10-point scale
	* Functional assessments: limited mobility (American College of Rheumatology [ACR] score ≥ 3)

**Medical Necessity Criteria**

To determine medical necessity for an MRI, the following criteria must be met:

1. **Patient Selection Criteria**: The patient must have:
	* A primary diagnosis of osteoarthritis, ligament sprain, or meniscal tear
	* Failed conservative treatment within the past 12 weeks
2. **Failed Prior Treatments**: The patient must have:
	* Received conservative treatment for a minimum of 12 weeks without improvement
	* Experienced significant pain or functional impairment (e.g., ACR score ≥ 4)
3. **Contraindications to Conservative Care**: The patient must have:
	* Severe comorbidities that preclude conservative treatment (e.g., diabetes, hypertension)

**Exclusion Criteria**

The following conditions will not be covered under this policy:

1. **Active Infections**: The patient must not have an active infection in the knee joint.
2. **Pregnancy (if Applicable)**: Pregnant patients will require prior authorization for MRI services.
3. **Severe Comorbidities**: Patients with severe comorbidities (e.g., diabetes, hypertension) may be excluded from coverage if conservative treatment is deemed ineffective or contraindicated.
4. **Lack of Conservative Treatment**: The patient must have attempted conservative treatment for a minimum of 12 weeks before being eligible for an MRI.
5. **Cosmetic Indications**: The patient's condition must meet the medical necessity criteria above; cosmetic indications will not be covered.

**Documentation Requirements**

The following documentation is required to support the need for an MRI:

1. **History and Physical Examination**: A detailed history and physical examination performed by the treating physician.
2. **Diagnostic Test Results**: Diagnostic test results (e.g., X-rays, EMG).
3. **Treatment Logs**: Treatment logs documenting conservative treatment efforts.
4. **Specialist Consultations**: Specialist consultations with orthopedic or rheumatology specialists.

**Authorization Details**

1. **Valid For**: This policy is valid for 90 days from the date of authorization.
2. **Requires Peer-to-Peer Review**: If denied, a peer-to-peer review will be required to ensure that the decision not to authorize coverage was made in accordance with this policy.
