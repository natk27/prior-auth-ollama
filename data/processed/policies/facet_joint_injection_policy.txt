PRIOR AUTHORIZATION POLICY
======================================================================

Procedure: Facet Joint Injection
CPT Code: 64493

======================================================================

**Insurance Policy Document**

**Procedure Overview**

* Procedure Name: Facet Joint Injection
* CPT Code: 64493
* Clinical Description: A minimally invasive procedure to inject a steroid medication into the facet joint to reduce inflammation, relieve pain, and improve mobility in patients with chronic low back pain or neck pain.

**Coverage Criteria (Must meet ALL)**

1. **Conservative Treatment Requirements**
	* The patient must have completed at least 12 weeks of physical therapy, including:
		+ 6 weeks of supervised exercise and stretching
		+ 3 weeks of manual therapy and modalities (heat, cold, electrical stimulation)
		+ 3 weeks of pain management education and self-management strategies
2. **Symptom Duration Requirements**
	* The patient must have experienced symptoms of chronic low back or neck pain for a minimum of 6 weeks prior to procedure initiation.
3. **Clinical Documentation Required**
	* The patient's medical record must include:
		+ A comprehensive history and physical examination report within the past 12 months
		+ Results from diagnostic tests, including:
			- Radiographs (X-rays)
			- Computed Tomography (CT) scans or Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) scans of the spine within the past 6 months
			- Nerve root injections or nerve blocks within the past 3 months
		+ Treatment logs, including:
			- Physical therapy treatment plans and progress notes
			- Pain management medication lists and dosages
			- Modalities used (heat, cold, electrical stimulation)
4. **Imaging Requirements**
	* Radiographs (X-rays) of the spine must be performed within the past 12 months.
	* Computed Tomography (CT) scans or Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) scans of the spine must be performed within the past 6 months.
5. **Severity Criteria**
	* The patient's pain must be measured on a numerical rating scale (NRS) of at least 4/10, indicating moderate to severe pain.
	* Functional assessments must demonstrate:
		+ Decreased range of motion in the affected joint
		+ Impaired balance or gait

**Medical Necessity Criteria**

1. **Patient Selection Criteria**
	* Patients with chronic low back or neck pain that has not responded to conservative treatment.
	* Patients who have failed at least 2 courses of physical therapy and/or medication management.
2. **Failed Prior Treatments**
	* The patient must have:
		+ Failed at least one course of medication (oral steroids, NSAIDs)
		+ Failed at least two courses of physical therapy
3. **Contraindications to Conservative Care**
	* Patients with:
		+ Active infections (e.g., sepsis, osteomyelitis)
		+ Pregnancy (if applicable)
		+ Severe comorbidities (e.g., diabetes, cardiovascular disease)

**Exclusion Criteria**

1. **Active Infections**
2. **Pregnancy (if applicable)**
3. **Severe Comorbidities**
4. **Lack of Conservative Treatment**
5. **Cosmetic Indications**

**Documentation Requirements**

1. **History and Physical Examination**
	* The patient's medical record must include a comprehensive history and physical examination report within the past 12 months.
2. **Diagnostic Test Results**
	* Radiographs (X-rays) of the spine must be performed within the past 12 months.
	* Computed Tomography (CT) scans or Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) scans of the spine must be performed within the past 6 months.
3. **Treatment Logs**
	* Physical therapy treatment plans and progress notes must be included.
4. **Specialist Consultations**
	* The patient's medical record must include consultation reports from:
		+ Primary care physician
		+ Specialist (e.g., orthopedic surgeon, pain management specialist)

**Authorization Details**

1. **Valid for:** 90 days from the date of authorization.
2. **Requires Peer-to-Peer Review if Denied:**
	* If the procedure is denied, a peer-to-peer review must be completed within 30 days to determine whether the denial was reasonable and well-supported.
